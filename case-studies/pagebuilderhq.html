The Challenge

Page builders solve the wrong problem.

Existing page builders give you a blank canvas and a drag-and-drop editor. Sounds great in theory. In practice, you spend three hours nudging boxes around until your page looks "close enough." Then it loads in 4 seconds and scores 40 on Lighthouse.

The alternative? Pay a designer $2,000. Or use Carrd and look like everyone else.

The gap: a tool that produces great-looking, fast-loading landing pages without forcing users into design work. Something opinionated enough to look good by default, flexible enough to fit real businesses.

What I Built

A self-serve landing page builder. No drag-and-drop in sight.

The Core Product

PageBuilderHQ flips the page builder model. Instead of a blank canvas, users pick from curated templates — each designed with the same craft as a custom build. Then they fill in a form: headline, description, CTA, images. The builder handles layout, typography, spacing, responsive behavior, and code output.

Live preview updates in real time as users type. No design decisions required. Just content.

The Template Library

12+ curated landing page templates, each with a distinct visual identity:

  • Midnight Consulting — Dark, editorial advisory theme
  • Blueprint — Bold design-at-scale look for agencies
  • Clean SaaS — Minimal light theme for software products
  • Bold Agency — High-contrast creative studio aesthetic
  • Aurora Startup — Gradient-rich modern startup vibes
  • Coral Lifestyle — Warm, organic design for people-first brands
  • Neon Event — Dramatic dark theme with vibrant event energy

Not 500 templates that all look the same. A handful that each look genuinely different.

Publishing & Hosting

One-click publish. Custom domain support with automatic SSL. Global CDN. Pages go live in seconds — clean static HTML, sub-second load times, perfect Lighthouse scores. No bloated JavaScript, no unnecessary tracking.

Built-in Forms & Analytics

Every page includes a working form. Launch plan: 100 submissions/month. Scale plan: unlimited. Submissions emailed to the user and stored in a dashboard. Built-in analytics so users can track performance without bolting on third-party scripts.

The Takeaway

The best tool is the one that doesn't make you think.

Most page builders optimize for flexibility. PageBuilderHQ optimizes for output. Users don't want to become designers — they want a page that looks good, loads fast, and converts. That's it.

The constraint is the feature. Curated templates, form-based input, opinionated design. Less freedom, better results.
